> Acidification on Calcifiers

Due to increased CO2 in the water, and therefore an increase of H+ ions, the amount of available CO32- ions present in the environment decreases. All calcifiers struggle to produce CaCO3 in conditions of low pH, hence why population growth among calcifiers has declined in recent years. Focusing on mollusks specifically, there are three primary impacts corresponding to acidification.

Firstly, growth and calcification of larval and adult mollusks both see changes, often in that shell sizes begin to vary from the average in a population. Secondly, reproduction of mollusks goes down, while inheritance of traits that allow tolerance for acid goes up. Thirdly, the mollusk immune system sees negative impacts due to more energy allocated to maintaining a steady internal pH.

However, results are often contradictory on this topic, with many studies countering each other with polar results. What all researchers agree on is that there is high chance of mollusk adaptation to lower pH.
